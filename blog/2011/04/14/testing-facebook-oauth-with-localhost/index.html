Testing Facebook OAuth Using Localhost

Apr 14th, 2011

I’ve been toying around with the Facebook API’s including authenticating a user using OAuth. One thing that initially bugged me was having to deploy my app to a real server (e.g. not my local dev box). This was the approach I took because Facebook needed to be able to “callback” to my application once the user had signed in.

It didn’t take long for me to look for some alternative, or method of testing/running things from localhost. Actually, there’s nothing really to it. All you have to do is configure your “app” on Facebook with the appropriate settings.

  1. Go to the developers page on Facebook
  2. Click on the name of your app under “My Apps” (on the right side of the page)
  3. Click on “Edit Settings”
  4. Click on “Website” (on the left side of the page)
  5. Enter the following details:
    • Site URL: http://localhost[:port]
    • Site Domain: localhost
  6. Click “Save Changes”

Give Facebook a few minutes to update and you should be good to go!
