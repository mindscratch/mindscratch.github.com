Hackergarten Follow-Up

Nov 9th, 2010

After posting the interview with Hamlet D’Arcy about Hackergarten there was some intersting discussion that stirred up.

Here’s some key take-aways I found intersting and perhaps useful:

Groups

  • Small groups 2 (preffered) to 6 (max)
  • Each group works on different tasks

Tasking

  • Choose small tasks, very small to start.
  • Complete the task in a single night.
  • Examples
    • Fix documentation (add a couple paragraphs to a user guide, fill-in javadocs)
    • Minor refactorings
    • Small plugins, etc.
  • The point is to release something, not to “just code”, you want to be a contributor, not just talk about it

Meeting Preparation and Structure

  • Meet once a month (seems to be the “right” frequency)
  • No defined structure, just start when people are ready and stop when people have had enough.
  • Might be worth defining some timebox (i.e. 8pm - 11pm) to help keep the meeting focused.
  • Choose a topic as early as possible to give everyone plenty of time to prepare
  • computer must have tools installed and functioning ahead of time
    • IDE, build tool, etc
    • a lot of time can be wasted configuring the IDE, installing plugins, etc
  • 15 minute demo at the start to kick-off the night (or day)

Common Pitfalls

taken directly from Hamlet’s response

  • Spend too much time setting up IDEs. Having a pre-made .ipr helps a lot.
  • Spend too much time messing with Version Control. For a 4 hour development project, emailing .java files back and forth is sometimes the best thing to do.
  • Spend too much time talking about what we can do. I think you need to give the night a theme. “GPars”, or “Gradle”. Just “Meetup” is not enough
  • Start a too-large project.
