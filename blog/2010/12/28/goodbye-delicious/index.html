Goodbye, Delicious. and Why I Don’t Think I’ll Miss Ya.

Dec 28th, 2010

I started using Delicious almost 6 years ago to bookmark favorite sites, articles I found interesting, useful tips, tricks, etc. For a long time I used the Firefox extension to easily save a URL into the Delicious ether. When I moved to Chrome I used a bookmarklet to do the job. Over the years I’ve continued to bookmark things, until I read the recent news.

It appears Yahoo! is going to discontinue the service, perhaps sell it, perhaps figure out a new home for it, who knows. At first I was bummed, wondering what I was going to do with all my bookmarks. I’ve spent years bookmarking and even recently tweeting my links. At the time of this post I’ve collected 2,085 links covering all sorts of topics from programming to automobiles. My initial motivation for using Delicious (originally, http://delicio.us) was to bookmark links for later research. It turns out that I never really did that. Instead, I added bookmarks and never looked back.

So today, I say “Goodbye Delicious”. I’ve exported my bookmarks, just in case (of what..who knows), and imported them into Google Bookmarks. For now, I think I’m done bookmarking the “old” way. I think I’ll probably stick to using Twitter (maybe Buzz…although Buzz hasn’t gotten a lot of traction yet) to share links I find useful.

Did/do you use Delcious? What are your thoughts or plans moving forward?
