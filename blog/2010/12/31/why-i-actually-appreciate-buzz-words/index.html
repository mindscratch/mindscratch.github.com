Why I Actually Appreciate Buzz Words

Dec 31st, 2010

Buzz words in the tech. industry can catch on like wild fire, hence the reason they get coined as being “buzz” words. As a software craftsman (side note: one step to becoming a software craftsman is to refer to yourself as a software craftsman. Corey Haines posted a link to a video where he explains how to become a software craftsman. I can’t find it right now because “Loading Tweets seems to be taking a while. Twitter may be over capacity or experiencing a momentary hiccup.”) I’ve learned to enjoy and actually appreciate these buzz words. Certainly they get over used, misused and misunderstood. But there is an upside.

Once the non-tech. people that are considered management (or some other position of “power”) in a company/organization catch wind of the buzz words, they become embedded in their vocabulary. The advantage for developers is that this allows us to throw around the buzz words to help get something we might need or want. For example, I’m intersted in attending a MongoDB event. At some point when it comes to a city near me I’ll likely take a day off to attend. In my particular case I won’t have to get some sort of approval to take off but stay with me for a minute. Imagine I needed to get approval from some non-tech. person of “power” to attend the event. If I talk about MongoDB they’d likely not understand what MongoDB is, nor would they understand its potential relevance. Instead, I could talk about attending an event regarding NoSQL. It’s much more likely they’ve heard the term NoSQL and perhaps have also heard about other companies/projects using it. In this situation the buzz word, NoSQL, helps provide leverage to the “power-less” developer to get the approval needed to attend the event.

If you need some more buzz words to help you out here are a few:

  • NoSQL
  • AJAX
  • Web 2.0
  • Web 3.0 (why not)
  • HTML5 (it’s a real thing of course but is very buzzy)
  • Mashup
  • Cloud (almost forgot this one)
  • UX

Happy new years!
